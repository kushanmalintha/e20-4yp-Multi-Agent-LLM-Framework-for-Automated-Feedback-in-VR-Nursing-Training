HISTORY TAKING GUIDELINES
Post-Operative Wound Care

ESSENTIAL CONVERSATION ELEMENTS

1. INTRODUCTION & IDENTITY VERIFICATION
- Introduce yourself as a nursing student
- Ask patient's name, age, and address
- Purpose: Prevent wrong-patient errors, establish rapport

2. ALLERGY ASSESSMENT (CRITICAL)
- Ask about medication allergies
- Ask about dressing/tape/latex allergies
- Purpose: Prevent allergic reactions, guide material selection

3. PAIN ASSESSMENT
- Ask if patient has pain at wound site
- Ask about pain severity (scale 0-10 or mild/moderate/severe)
- Purpose: Ensure comfort, identify potential complications

4. MEDICAL HISTORY
- Ask about current medical conditions
- Ask about recent surgery/procedure
- Purpose: Understand wound context and risk factors

5. PROCEDURE EXPLANATION
- Explain what you will do (assess, clean, dress the wound)
- Explain why it's needed (prevent infection, promote healing)
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Purpose: Reduce anxiety, gain cooperation

6. PATIENT COMFORT
- Ask if patient needs anything before starting
- Check if patient is comfortable
- Purpose: Prevent interruptions, demonstrate patient-centered care

COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES
- Speak clearly and respectfully
- Listen actively to patient responses
- Do not rush or assume information
- Base all clinical decisions on information gathered

EVALUATION BASIS
If the student does not ask about something, it is considered not done.
Patient safety requires complete information gathering.
